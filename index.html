A bit about me

I am currently a research assistant at the Translational Disease Systems Biology Group, Center for Protein Research founded by Novo Nordisk Foundation. My research mostly revolves around the development of in-house tools that handle large biobank data.

My work interests revolve around software development and deep learning prediction models. At the beginning of my MSc studies I became part of the Bruank group where I started developing pipelines which can be used on the Copenhagen Hospital Biobank and the Danish Blood Donors Study cohorts.

I am focused on uncovering associations between genomic and phenotypic data. In 2021 I developed a pipeline for performing automated Phenome Wide Association Studies on biobank datasets. Additionally as a part of my MSc studies I also performed an analysis on the genomic effects for SARS-COV-2 on a population of Danish blood donors. More recently I have been working on integration of different formats of genetic information to a deep learning framework and evaluation of prediction models.

In my free time I enjoy training my body through weight-lifting or Brazilian Jiu Jitsu as part of my daily routine, but my evenings are mostly focused on socializing with my friends or colleagues over a game of pool and some beers.
